A good resume

A good curriculum vitae (CV) is a crucial tool for job seekers to showcase their skills, experience, and achievements to potential employers.

To create an effective CV, it's important to tailor it to the job you are applying for. This means including relevant keywords and phrases that match the job description. Using clear, concise language and bullet points can also make your CV easier to read and understand..

There are different types of CVs, including:

  • Chronological
  • Functional
  • Combination

A chronological CV is the most commonly used format and lists a candidate's work experience in reverse chronological order, starting with the most recent job. This type of CV is useful when applying for jobs in the same field where the candidate has a solid work history.

A functional CV focuses on a candidate's skills and achievements, rather than their work history. This format is useful for candidates who have limited work experience or are changing careers.

A combination CV combines elements of both chronological and functional CVs, highlighting a candidate's skills and achievements while also providing a chronological work history.

The Harvard CV format is one of the most popular and widely used formats for academic and research-related job applications

Clarity and readability

The Harvard CV format emphasizes clarity and readability, making it easy for employers to quickly scan and identify key information such as your education, work experience, publications, and research interests.

Professional and formal appearance

The Harvard CV format has a professional and formal appearance that is well-suited for academic and research-related positions. It includes clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points, which make it easy to read and understand.

Flexibility and customization

The Harvard CV format is flexible and can be customized to highlight your unique qualifications and achievements. It allows you to organize your information in a way that best showcases your strengths and experience.

Focus on research and academic achievements

The Harvard CV format is particularly well-suited for highlighting research and academic achievements. It includes sections such as publications, presentations, and research experience, which are important for academic and research-related positions.

Recognition and credibility

The Harvard CV format is recognized and respected by many employers in the academic and research fields, which can lend credibility to your application

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is a software application used by employers to streamline the recruitment and hiring process. It automates the initial screening of job applications, allowing employers to efficiently filter through a large volume of resumes and cover letters. When you apply for a job online, your application will likely be scanned by an ATS before it reaches human eyes. The ATS scans your resume and cover letter for specific keywords and phrases that match the requirements of the job posting. Therefore, it's crucial to customize your resume and cover letter to the job posting and include relevant keywords and phrases that are likely to be picked up by the ATS. If your resume doesn't contain these keywords, it may be automatically rejected by the system and never seen by a human recruiter. Hence, optimizing your application for ATS will increase the chances of your resume and cover letter being seen by a human recruiter and potentially lead to an interview.

To optimize your resume for ATS, use a clean, easy-to-read format that is compatible with the system. Avoid using images, graphics, and fancy fonts that may not be recognized by the ATS. Ensure that the important information is in text format. Use headings and bullet points to organize your information and use relevant keywords and phrases throughout your resume and cover letter.
